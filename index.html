The Details

Summary/Abstract

Our team has been assigned the task of designing, assembling, and validating an electric drivetrain capable of being integrated into next season's competition car for CSU’s Formula SAE. We aim to engineer a highly energy efficient, and cost effective drivetrain that will perform well under every FSAE competition category. We will be validating our project on the existing “Warthog” chassis that was donated to the CSU FSAE Club by CU Boulder in 2009. Our project will consist of completing subsystems within a block level diagram structured around the rear-end of the vehicle. This will include researching and designing individual components so that all subsystems can be seamlessly integrated together. Our project will allow for complete control from the accelerator pedal, to the motor controller, and all the way down to the motors themselves.

Project Background

Formula SAE (FSAE) is a collegiate design competition sponsored by the Society of Automotive Engineers (SAE) in which students design, manufacture and compete in a formula-style race car. FSAE is not only the largest international intercollegiate competition, but also the biggest annual engineering competition worldwide. The first iteration in 1978 called the “SAE Mini-Indy” competition attracted some of the best engineering students from around the world. Colorado State University’s FSAE team has named themselves Ram Racing, and joined 500 teams from around the world in competition. Six competitions are hosted each year, two of which are held in the United States, FSAE Michigan and FSAE West. More specifically, hosted in Brooklyn, Michigan, and Lincoln, Nebraska

Constraits and Goals

- Validate our EV design on the "Warthog" chassis, to be applied to a future competition car. - Meet 2021-2022 competition requirements - Offer smooth transition for future teams to continue the project with proper documentation
